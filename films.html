Empty Bowls

Role: Filmmaker

Process

For this film, I collaborated with a team of 5 student filmmakers to produce a short documentary addressing food insecurity in Iowa, spotlighting the Empty Bowls event as a community-driven solution to inspire audience engagement. During this project I:

• Co-led a 45-minutes interview with Empty Bowls Grinnell founders Jane Mertens and Cecilia Knight, shaping the narrative through their insights.

• Managed production workflow, coordinating team meetings resulting in filming 200+ A-roll and B-roll clips.

• Structuring 30+ minutes of interview material, adjusted sound, improved flow through compositing and editing techniques, and applyed color-correction for the final cut in Premier Pro.

• Worked with lavalier microphones, zoom recorders, professional cameras, and lights to achieve the high-quality picture and sound.

 

J.B. Grinnell Is Back

Role: Film Director

Process

For this film, I co-directed a 10-minutes horror-comedy film and oversaw all creative and technical aspects of a supernatural comedy short film, guiding a team of 15 cast and crew members from pre-production to post-production. During this project I:

• Storyboarded and documented 100+ shots for 10+ scenes, shaping the film's visual storytelling and comedic tone to develop J.B. Grinnell's character, balancing history with modern comedic appeal to resonate with the target audience.

• Oversaw shot composition, camera movements, and blocking, collaborating with the Directors of Photography while managing on-location problem-solving for an efficient workflow.

• Directed the collaborative work of actors, sound, lighting, and cinematographers crews accumulating 90+ minutes of useable footage over 4 days for the editor.
